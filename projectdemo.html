My Project (demo)

NHS waiting lists have long been a politically salient topic. In the Summer, an analysis published by the IFS found that more than 13 million patients may be awaiting treatment by July 2022.

My project looks at the relationship between deprivation (as a proxy for socioeconomic status) and the pandemics effect on NHS waiting lists.

Unsurprisingly, as resources shifted toward coronavirus, the number of elective treatments fell.

However, this only tells part of the story.

Comparing 2019 and 2020 patient patterns, there is a 6 million fall in new pathways started.

It is thought that during lockdown many patients who should have come forwards to seek medical attention did not.
