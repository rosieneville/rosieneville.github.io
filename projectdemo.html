My Project (demo)

NHS waiting lists have long been a politically salient topic. In the Summer, an analysis published by the IFS found that more than 13 million patients may be awaiting treatment by July 2022 .

My project looks at the relationship between deprivation and the pandemics effect on NHS waiting lists.

Drop in elective treatments.

When the pandemic hit, a lot of planned elective care had to be postponed in order to deal with the pressures of coronavirus. This led to a sharp fall in completed pathways. In 2020, compared with 2019, over 4 million fewer elective treatment pathways were completed.

However, this only tells part of the story.

Drop in new pathways started.

Additionally, there was a sharp fall in the number of people starting new treatment pathways. In 2020, compared with 2019, almost 6 million new pathways were started. This can be thought of as the ‘missing patients’ as represents a key challenge in tackling the backlog caused by the pandemic.
